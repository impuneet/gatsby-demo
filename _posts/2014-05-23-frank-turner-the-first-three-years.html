Thoughts:

This song is a cover of the first single from Frank Turner's old band, Million Dead. That band was post-hardcore. I heard the original, and I frankly don't like it as much--I found that the vocals got buried underneath the instruments, while in Frank Turner's stuff, his voice is the the most prevalent instrument and always acts as the melody.

It's interesting how complicated these lyrics are compared to Frank's new stuff. I remember reading an interview that Frank was focusing on simple lyrics and easily getting the meaning across rather than the complicated lyrics of Million Dead. I find it harder to connect emotionally with this song, because I don't know what the point is up until the last minute. It took a long time to warm up to it.

I often listen to this song straight for hours upon hours. The rhythm of the song, both with the flow of the vocals and the guitar/drums that make up the harmony have my absolutely obsessed.

Frank Turner songs are like a symphony in 5 minutes. A common theme is this this build-up through the song until the last minute is like this rush of emotion and harmony. Once it ends, it's not enough--so I repeat it again and again and again. I only stop when I get interrupted.
